QR Resto Hub - UX Design Directions

Modern Epicurean direction plus six practical directions for customer, restaurant, and platform surfaces.

Brand Adaptation: Modern Epicurean

Use the Google Stitch reference as the current UI direction: quiet luxury for digital dining with cream, cocoa, orange, olive, and food-first presentation.

Reference alignment

Bring forward

Customer-facing surfaces should feel calm, crisp, and food-first. Let dishes, table/chair context, QR assets, and live status lead the page while the interface stays quiet.

  • Use real dish or restaurant imagery where it helps ordering confidence.
  • Prefer cream surfaces, cocoa ink, tonal layering, barely visible cocoa separators, and glassy sticky overlays over decorative effects.
  • Use orange as the primary CTA/action color for ordering and dashboard actions.
  • Use olive only for dietary tags, sustainable/health markers, and subtle confirmed states.

Do not copy

The reference is not a request to become Apple. QR Resto Hub remains an operational restaurant SaaS with mobile QR ordering.

  • No Apple branding, SF Pro typography, Apple navigation, or device/product-page copy.
  • No negative letter spacing; keep Plus Jakarta Sans headings, Inter body text, and documented token rules.
  • No sterile cool-toned wellness palette or sparse marketing layout for admin boards, account tables, or live workflows.

Direction 1: Mobile Ordering First

A thumb-friendly diner flow that feels like opening a polished menu.

Best for customer QR UX

Use this when

  • The customer flow must feel instant and app-less.
  • Dish browsing, add-ons, and order notes need to stay simple.
  • Primary actions must stay reachable on small screens.

  • Connection states need to be visible but calm.

Direction 6: Recovery and Confidence States

Clear handling for reconnect, invalid QR, blocked ads, invalid moves, and cancellation races.

Best for resilience UX

Use this when

  • The system needs to preserve trust when something goes wrong.
  • Rejected actions should feel handled, not broken.
  • Ad-block recovery needs to be firm without feeling hostile.
